# Chapter 5: Understanding STRAT Tokenomics

## What is Tokenomics?

Tokenomics = Token + Economics

It's the economic system that governs a cryptocurrency, including:
- Total supply
- Distribution mechanisms
- Inflation/deflation
- Utility and use cases
- Incentive structures

## Supply Dynamics

### Inflation Mechanisms (Token Creation)

**Staking Rewards**:
New STRAT issued to validators
- Current rate: ~5% annual inflation
- Decreases over time

**Ecosystem Grants**:
Funds released for development
- Controlled by DAO
- Vesting schedules

### Deflation Mechanisms (Token Burning)

**Transaction Fee Burning**:
Portion of gas fees permanently destroyed
- Burns approximately 2-3% annually
- Reduces circulating supply

**Buyback and Burn**:
Protocol uses revenue to purchase and burn STRAT
- Quarterly burns
- Transparent process

**Penalty Burns**:
Slashed tokens from malicious validators
- Security mechanism
- Returns to all honest stakers

### Net Effect
**Target**: Near-zero long-term inflation or slight deflation

## Token Metrics to Monitor

### Market Cap
`Price × Circulating Supply`

**Categories**:
- < $100M: Small cap
- $100M - $1B: Mid cap
- > $1B: Large cap

### Fully Diluted Valuation (FDV)
`Price × Max Supply`

### Circulating Supply
Tokens currently in circulation (excludes locked/vested)

### Trading Volume

- High volume = High liquidity

### Market Dominance

## Economic Security

### 51% Attack Resistance
Cost to attack network:
```
Attack Cost = 51% of Staked STRAT × STRAT Price
```

**Current security**: $XXM to attack (economically infeasible)

### Sybil Resistance
Staking requirements prevent cheap attacks:
- Must lock real capital
- Slashing penalties
- Reputation system

### Oracle Manipulation
Decentralized price feeds prevent:

- Price manipulation
